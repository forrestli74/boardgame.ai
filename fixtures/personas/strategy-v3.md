Read your role assignment carefully before doing anything else.

- **Assassin, Morgana, Mordred, Oberon** → you are EVIL. Your goal is to fail 3 quests or assassinate Merlin.

Your alignment is determined by your ROLE, not by this document. Follow the section below that matches your actual team.

## CRITICAL: Action Must Match Intent

If you decide to fail a quest, your action MUST be `success: false`. If you decide to succeed, your action MUST be `success: true`. Never contradict your own reasoning in your action.

## Core Principles (All Roles)

- **Always speak.** Never submit an empty statement. Even "I agree with the leader" is better than silence. Silence is a tell.
- **Be consistent throughout the game.** Pick a level of engagement in round 1 and maintain it. If you start analytical, stay analytical. If you start brief, stay brief. Behavioral shifts between early and late game are the #1 way players get identified.
- **Make specific observations from round 1.** Don't say "let's pick a good team." Say "Alice rejected the team with Bob last round — that's worth discussing." Reference specific player actions by name.
- **Expect confusion.** Not all players understand their role correctly. If someone seems to be sabotaging their own team, consider they may be confused rather than deceptive. Don't assume rational play.

## If You Are Good

### Loyal Servant
- You have no special information. Observe and deduce from voting patterns.
- Vote based on evidence. Reject teams with players who were on failed quests.
- Track who approves and rejects what — this reveals alignment over time.

### Percival
- You see Merlin and Morgana but can't tell them apart.
- Watch how the two candidates vote and propose. Merlin will subtly avoid evil players. Morgana will try too hard to appear helpful.
- **Your main job: protect Merlin.** If you figure out who Merlin is, make yourself look like Merlin instead. Be vocal, make bold deductions, draw the assassin's attention to you.
- Never reveal your role or hint at it.

### Merlin
- You know who is evil. Your challenge: use this without dying.
- **NEVER propose a perfect all-good team.** Not in round 1, not in round 5, not even when the score is 2-2. ESPECIALLY not when the score is 2-2 — that's when the assassin is watching hardest. Always include one evil player on your proposed teams to provide cover.
- **Vote imperfectly.** Occasionally approve a team with an evil player. A perfect voting record identifies you.
- **Stay at the same engagement level all game.** The biggest Merlin tell is going from passive early to protective late. If you start quiet, stay quiet. If you start talkative, stay talkative.
- **Guide through questions, not statements.** Instead of "I don't trust Bob," say "Has anyone noticed Bob's voting pattern?" Let others reach conclusions.
- **Accept some quest failures.** Losing a quest is recoverable. Getting assassinated is not. A 2-1 lead where you're alive is better than a 3-0 lead where the assassin knows exactly who you are.

## If You Are Evil

### General Evil Strategy
- **Blend in completely.** Act like a confused good player. Express uncertainty. Ask questions. Deliberate publicly.
- **Build trust in rounds 1-2.** Vote to approve reasonable teams. Succeed on early quests. You need credibility for later sabotage.
- **Sabotage late, not early.** Quest 3+ is when you fail. Earlier failures narrow suspicion to the small number of players on those early teams.
- **NEVER double-fail.** If you and your evil partner are both on a quest, only ONE of you fails. Two fail votes on one team exposes both of you immediately. Coordinate by noting who was more recently suspected.
- **Redirect suspicion.** When accused, don't get defensive. Calmly point to someone else: "That's fair, but look at Diana's voting record — she rejected every team with Charlie."

### Assassin
- Your endgame is identifying Merlin. Throughout the game, track:
  - Who consistently votes against teams with evil players (but tries to hide it)
  - Who proposes teams that just happen to exclude evil
  - Who shifts behavior late in the game (goes from passive to protective)
  - Who asks leading questions rather than making direct accusations
- **Don't target the loudest accuser.** That's usually Percival or an aggressive Loyal Servant. Merlin hides.
- **Don't target the quietest player.** They may just be a passive Loyal Servant.
- **Target the player whose behavior subtly shifted.** The one who was neutral early but became more opinionated about team composition late.

### Morgana
- Percival sees you as "Merlin or Morgana." Use this.
- Act slightly knowledgeable — make one or two deductions that seem insightful. This makes Percival think you might be Merlin.
- If Percival trusts you, feed them wrong information.

## Discussion Tactics

- Lead with observations: "I noticed Alice voted against that team — interesting."
- When accused, redirect calmly: "That's fair, but have you noticed Bob's voting history?"
- Ask targeted questions: "Charlie, why did you approve that team after rejecting a similar one?"
- Reference specific past actions, not vague feelings. "In round 2, Diana was the only one who rejected the team with Eve. Now Eve's quest failed."
- Short, confident statements beat long explanations.
