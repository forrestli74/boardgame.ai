# Avalon Strategy Guide

## General Principles

- Pay attention to voting patterns. Players who consistently approve suspicious teams may be evil.
- Track quest results and who was on each team. If a quest fails, one or more team members are evil.
- Use discussion to share observations and build trust. Be specific — vague claims are unconvincing.
- The reject vote is powerful. Don't approve teams you're unsure about. A rejected team costs little.
- Late-game information is worth more than early-game information. Be patient.

## Good Team

### Merlin

You know who the evil players are, but you must hide this knowledge. If good wins 3 quests, the assassin gets to guess who Merlin is — if they're right, evil wins instead.

- Never directly accuse evil players. Instead, steer the conversation indirectly — support good players, raise doubts about evil ones without being obvious.
- Vote reject on teams that include evil players, but occasionally approve a borderline team so your pattern isn't too clean.
- If Mordred is in the game, remember you cannot see them. One evil player is hidden from you.
- Support Percival subtly. If Percival seems to trust you, don't confirm it publicly — that reveals you both.
- In discussion, frame your objections as reasoning from public information (vote patterns, quest results) rather than private knowledge.

### Percival

You see two players marked as "Merlin or Morgana" but cannot tell which is which. Your job is to figure out who the real Merlin is and protect them.

- Watch how the two candidates behave. Merlin tends to have better intuition about who is evil. Morgana might push for teams that include evil players.
- Once you've identified the real Merlin, support their preferred teams without making it obvious you're following them.
- If the assassin is trying to identify Merlin, draw suspicion toward yourself. Better the assassin guesses you than the real Merlin.
- Don't reveal that you are Percival early. Your power is in quietly observing.

### Loyal Servant

You have no special information. Your strength is honest reasoning from public data.

- Track every vote and quest result carefully. Build a mental model of who might be evil.
- If a quest fails, narrow down which team members could be responsible.
- Trust players whose voting patterns are consistent with being good. Distrust players who approve teams that fail.
- In discussion, share your reasoning openly. Good players benefit from transparency — evil players don't.
- Don't be afraid to reject teams. A wrong approval (sending evil on a quest) is worse than a wrong rejection.

## Evil Team

### Assassin

You know who the other evil players are (except Oberon). Your special power: if good wins 3 quests, you get to guess Merlin for the win.

- Throughout the game, watch for Merlin. Look for the player whose objections are most accurate — they may have hidden knowledge.
- Players who consistently reject teams with evil players (without public justification) are Merlin candidates.
- In discussion, act like a good player. Build trust. Accuse other evil players occasionally to seem genuine — coordinate with your team on who takes the heat.
- On quests, decide carefully whether to fail or succeed. Sometimes passing a quest early builds trust for a critical fail later.

### Morgana

You appear as "Merlin or Morgana" to Percival. Your job is to confuse Percival and make the assassin's guess harder.

- Act like Merlin. Show good intuition. Make accurate-sounding accusations (you know who evil is, so accuse your own teammates strategically).
- If Percival seems to trust you over the real Merlin, you're succeeding — the assassin can now target the wrong person.
- In discussion, be confident and analytical. Merlin-like behavior draws Percival's trust and makes you a plausible Merlin to the assassin's eye.

### Mordred

Merlin cannot see you. You are the hidden evil player.

- You can be bolder than other evil players. Merlin won't object to your presence on teams.
- Use this advantage to get on quests and fail them when it matters.
- In early quests, vote and behave like a good player. Your cover is stronger than any other evil player's.
- Coordinate with visible evil players — they draw Merlin's attention while you operate freely.

### Oberon

You don't know who the other evil players are, and they don't know you. You're on your own.

- You must figure out who your evil teammates are from behavior, just like good players do.
- Be cautious about failing quests early — without coordination, you might fail a quest alongside another evil player, wasting two fails.
- In discussion, blend in with good players. Your isolation is a disadvantage but also means Merlin doesn't see you (if Mordred is not in the game, Merlin sees all evil except you — wait, Oberon IS visible to Merlin). Actually, Merlin sees Oberon. Your advantage is that evil players can't coordinate with you, which makes your behavior unpredictable.
- Focus on getting onto quest teams and failing at critical moments.

### Minion of Mordred

You know the other evil players (except Oberon). You're a generalist — support the evil team's plans.

- Coordinate with your evil teammates in voting. If the assassin or Morgana needs a quest seat, help them get approved.
- Take the heat when needed. If suspicion falls on a more important evil player (Assassin, Morgana), draw attention to yourself instead.
- Fail quests when instructed by the team's implicit strategy, but don't fail every quest you're on — that's too obvious.
